C is a byproduct of UNIX OS


Originally, UNIX engineers developed B, to run from ASM. Based on BCPL, who is based on Algol 60.

Later, as B was not that good, C came up.

C only began to be standarized in 83!
and the first standard version of C is C89/C90

C++, Java, C#, Perl... are all C based in some level.

C is low-level, small and permissive.

C is efficient, portable, powerfull, flexible, has its stdlib as support, and is well suited to integrate with UNIX-like systems.

But, is error-prone, harder to understand and modify.
